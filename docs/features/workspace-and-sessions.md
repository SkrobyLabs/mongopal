# Workspace, sessions & preferences

## What it does

Holds the whole exploration experience together. Multiple collection, document, schema, and
index views open as tabs that can be renamed, pinned, and drag-reordered, and the session is
restored when the app reopens. Databases and collections can be starred as
[favorites](../glossary.md) for quick sidebar access, and queries kept as
[saved queries](../glossary.md) alongside auto-recorded history. The entire app is
keyboard-navigable — sidebar tree, tab switching, bulk selection, and query history all have
shortcuts. Notifications collect into a history drawer, with actionable hints suggesting how
to recover from errors.

A settings surface tailors the experience: query limits and timeouts, editor behavior
(auto-format, word wrap, line numbers, column freezing), large-document truncation, the AI
model choice, and appearance — selectable and custom themes plus UI and monospace fonts.

## Why it exists

Real work is not one query in isolation — it is jumping between several collections and
connections while keeping your place. Tabs, session restore, and pervasive keyboard control
make that context-switching fast, and turn MongoPal from a document viewer into a workspace
a power user can drive without reaching for the mouse. The preferences let each user trade
safety limits against convenience and make long sessions comfortable.

## Constraints & trade-offs

- Restored tabs remember their query and view, but re-execute against the database on
  reopen rather than caching stale result sets.
- Notification history is retained for review; the visible toast stack is capped and grouped
  to avoid flooding.
